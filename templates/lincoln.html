Abraham Lincoln

16th President of the United States


If not for Abraham Lincoln (March 4, 1861 to April 15, 1865), who presided during the American Civil War, the U.S. might look completely different today. Lincoln guided the Union through four bloody years of conflict, abolished enslavement with the Emancipation Proclamation, and at the war's end laid the foundation for reconciliation with the defeated South. Lincoln did not live to see a fully reunited nation. He was assassinated by John Wilkes Booth in Washington, D.C., weeks before the Civil War officially concluded.
