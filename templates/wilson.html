Woodrow Wilson

28th President of the United States


Woodrow Wilson (March 4, 1913 to March 4, 1921) began his first term vowing to keep the nation out of foreign entanglements. But by his second term, Wilson did an about-face and led the U.S. into World War I. At the war's conclusion, Wilson began a vigorous campaign to create a global alliance to prevent future conflicts. The resulting League of Nations, a precursor to the United Nations, was largely hobbled by the United States' refusal to participate after rejecting the Treaty of Versailles.
